Volunteer roles & commitments

Choose the role that fits your skills — each role includes orientation, a simple schedule, and administrative support from ProtectUS.

Therapist / Psychologist

  • Commitment: 2–4 hours/week (tele-sessions or in-person).
  • Tasks: short-term counseling (4–8 sessions), crisis triage, staff training.
  • Support: case supervision & monthly reviews with clinical lead.

Volunteer Lawyer

  • Commitment: weekly or biweekly clinics (2–4 hours) or on-call advice.
  • Tasks: intake consultations, complaint preparation, representation referrals, legal workshops.
  • Support: case managers assist with paperwork and logistics.

Community / Support Volunteer

  • Commitment: flexible (events, awareness, translations).
  • Tasks: event help, hotline support (non-clinical), outreach in schools.
  • Support: training on safe listening and referral protocols.

Volunteer structure (at a glance)

Simple, role-based, and supported.

Therapist Flow

  1. Onboarding & confidentiality training
  2. 2–4 hrs/week clinical slots (booked via calendar)
  3. Provide short-term sessions & crisis triage
  4. Monthly supervision meeting (1 hour)

Lawyer Flow

  1. Onboarding & child-protection orientation
  2. Weekly/biweekly legal clinics (2–4 hrs)
  3. Assist with complaint prep & representation
  4. Record short awareness videos / workshops

How we recruit professionals

Professional Associations

We partner with Bar Associations and Psychology/ Psychiatry societies to circulate volunteering calls and present at meetings.

Universities & Training Centers

Partnerships with law and psychology faculties provide supervised internship opportunities and skilled volunteers.

Direct Outreach & Social Media

Targeted LinkedIn, Facebook groups, and professional mailing lists with clear role descriptions and easy sign-up links.

Frequently asked questions

Is volunteering paid?

No — roles are pro-bono. We offer training, recognition, and certificates. Travel expenses for in-person work can be reimbursed for approved activities.

How confidential are cases?

All volunteers sign confidentiality agreements and follow strict data protection and child safeguarding protocols.

What training is provided?

All volunteers attend an onboarding session: safe listening, child-protection law basics, referral pathways, and platform use.
