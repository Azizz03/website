How to report — step by step

If you suspect a child is at risk, follow these immediate, practical steps. ProtectUS can help at every step.

1

Ensure immediate safety

If a child is in immediate danger, call emergency services. Remove them from harm and ensure a trusted adult stays with them.

2

Listen & document

Listen calmly. Write down what the child says (dates, times, names) and save any messages, photos, or other evidence.

3

Seek medical & psychological care

Get medical attention when needed and arrange an immediate psychological triage if possible — ProtectUS can connect you to a therapist.

4

Report to authorities or school

We will explain which office to contact (police, child protection services, or school administration) and help prepare your complaint.

5

Legal follow-up & protection

Volunteer lawyers assist with filings, representation, and requesting protective measures if needed (restraining orders, school transfers).

Volunteer professionals — what to expect

Therapists (psychologists / psychiatrists)

Our clinical partners provide trauma-informed care. Practical commitments include:

  • Availability: each therapist offers 2–4 hours/week for individual consultations and group supervision.
  • Sessions: short-term counseling (4–8 sessions), crisis triage, and staff training.
  • Confidentiality: all sessions follow strict privacy and ethical standards.

Therapists join a monthly supervision meeting (1 hour) to review cases and ensure safe practice.

Lawyers (pro-bono volunteers)

Volunteer lawyers provide legal access and empowerment. Practical commitments include:

  • Availability: weekly or biweekly clinics (2–4 hours) or on-call urgent advice.
  • Services: initial consultations, complaint preparation, representation, and legal literacy workshops.
  • Awareness videos: participation in short, clear videos explaining rights and procedures for families and schools.

ProtectUS provides administrative support and case managers so volunteers focus on professional tasks.
